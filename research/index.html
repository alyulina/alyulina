I am interested in how populations of cells and organisms — from microbes to cancer — evolve over time. To study the dynamics of evolution, I develop theoretical models, analyze large sets of genomic data, and occasionally do experiments. I am currently involved in several projects, some of which are highlighted below.


Linkage equilibrium between rare alleles

Together with Zhiru Liu and Benjamin Good, I study the dynamics of linked alleles under mutation, selection, recombination, and drift. We use our theoretical predictions to gain insight into the patterns of recombination in bacterial populations.


Population genomics of the spoon-billed sandpiper

I am using simulations and mathematical modeling to learn how past changes in the population size of a critically endangered bird species contributed to its present-day genetic diversity. In collaboration with Raimundo Saona, Mateusz Konczal, and Fyodor Kondrashov.
